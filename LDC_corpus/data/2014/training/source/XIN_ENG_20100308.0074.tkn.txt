<P> 
Corporate bankruptcies up 2.5 percent on month in Japan 
< / P> 
<P> 
TOKYO , March 8 ( Xinhua ) -- The number of corporate bankruptcies 
in Japan increased by 2.5 percent in February compared to the 
previous month , according to a report released by the Tokyo Shoko 
Research on Monday .
< / P> 
<P> 
Compared to the previous year , however , bankruptcies were down 
17.2 percent , marking the seventh consecutive month of improving 
conditions as the nation looks to recover from dour economic 
conditions triggered by a credit crisis that started in the 
second half of 2008 .
< / P> 
<P> 
The companies that went bankrupt last month incurred debts of 439 
trillion yen ( 4.85 billion dollars ) , significantly lower than the 
previous month , when Japan Airlines ( JAL ) went into 
administration . The amount of debt incurred by companies going 
bankrupt was also 64.2 percent , smaller than that for the same 
month in 2009 .
< / P> 
<P> 
Sectors badly hit in February included building , service and 
communications . In February , Japanese wireless carrier Willcom 
Inc . declared bankruptcy with debts of 206 billion yen ( 2.3 
billion dollars ) .
< / P> 
<P> 
In recent months the government and Bank of Japan ( BOJ ) have put 
in place a series of measures in an attempt to stimulate growth 
and household spending , with varying success .
< / P> 
<P> 
Statistics released earlier on Monday showed the amount of loans 
taken out from Japanese institutions was down 1.5 percent from a 
year earlier , indicating that despite government measures , 
businesses are still over capacity and remain cautious about 
future investment .
< / P>
