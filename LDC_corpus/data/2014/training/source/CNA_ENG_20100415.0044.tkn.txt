<HEADLINE> 
Taiwanese businessman indicted for spying for China 
< / HEADLINE> 
<TEXT> 
<P> 
Taipei , April 15 ( CNA ) A Taiwanese businessman with operations in 
China was indicted by the Shihlin Prosecutors Office Thursday for 
spying for China , according to the indictment released that day .
< / P> 
<P> 
Ho Chih - chiang , a China - based Taiwanese businessman , had been 
recruited by China 's national security authorities since 2007 to 
collect Taiwanese national security secrets in return for financial 
subsidies and other privileges , the indictment said .
< / P> 
<P> 
Acting under the instructions of Chinese officials , Ho returned 
to Taiwan and tried to recruit a Taiwan National Security Bureau 
( NSB ) officer , surnamed Chao , to serve as a Chinese spy by collecting 
information related to the government 's policies on Falun Gong , 
Tibetan independence , Japan and diplomacy , between December 2008 and 
February this year .
< / P> 
<P> 
Ho also offered Chao US$ 20,000 , liquor and additional pay several 
times that of Chao 's retirement pension in exchange for information 
on NSB overseas deployments and its satellite communication routings , 
but this was rejected by Chao .
< / P> 
<P> 
Ho was indicted on charges of bribery and of violating laws on 
national security and on protection of national secrets .
( By Lin Chang - shun and Bear Lee )
